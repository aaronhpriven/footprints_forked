Describe the evidence


The medium of evidence is where the footprint is derived or deduced from, e.g., an extant copy with an owner’s signature, a library mark, a booklist/catalog/inventory, a reference to a book coming into someone’s possession in a paratext to another work.
This field is required.

The provenance of evidence describes where can one find the evidence now, e.g., a particular library, archive, a printed book, a journal article.
